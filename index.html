About

I’m a quantitative economist completing my Master’s in Quantitative Economics at UCLA. I build empirical models that are honest about what they can and can’t prove, whether that’s a rolling-window LASSO benchmark for consumer expectations, a VAR of monetary policy, or a head-to-head of linear and tree-based models on noisy daily credit data.

The collection below spans my graduate coursework and capstone research. Each project pairs economic theory with a real dataset and a reproducible analysis in Python, R, or SQL.

Focus Macro-finance, applied econometrics, ML for economics
Toolkit Python (pandas, statsmodels, scikit-learn, XGBoost, pyblp), R, SQL
Methods Time series & VAR, panel data, LASSO, structural IO, backtesting
Based in Los Angeles, CA

“I think economics, and this is what I’ve tried to impart, has a tremendous amount of human interest in it.” Paul Samuelson

Beyond Margins publishes student writing that explores economics through a humanistic lens: development, financial crises, trade, agriculture, and climate change, examining how economic forces play out in specific cultural and geographic contexts. I built it to pair academic rigor with accessible storytelling about the economy.
